THE TRAINING SESSIONS | Closing the workshop

Session 18

FINAL REFLECTION AND EVALUATION

Goals of this session

  • To help the participants consider what is important about what they have just learned and how it might be helpful to them. 
  • To collect feedback from participants on the training and on the knowledge gained. 

Type of activity

Plenary session.

Timing

10 minutes for final reflection; 15 to 20 minutes for the evaluation.

Explain the purpose of the session.

Invite the participants to get into a comfortable position with their eyes closed. 

Ask the participants to reflect quietly for about 5 minutes on what is important about what they have just learned and how it might be helpful to them.

Next, say a keyword or phrase that relates to the main ideas that have been covered in the training, and ask the participants to reflect on the word for a couple of minutes. 

Repeat one or two more keywords or phrases, leaving a couple of minutes for reflection each time. 

Gather the group into a circle and invite them to share what they believe are the most important or valuable aspects of the ideas they have just reflected on, and how they can best use these ideas in their own situation.

What you need

Resources

  • For the final reflection: No resources are needed.
  • For the post-training evaluation: Evaluation questionnaire

Tips and comments

  • For the final reflection, the facilitator can choose a different technique to facilitate the reflection according to the type of group or culture.
  • The evaluation can also be done in plenary asking the participants to evaluate the training in terms of structure and content. 
  • The evaluation sheet can be modified by adding or deleting questions as considered appropriate and relevant.
