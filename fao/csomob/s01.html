THE METHODOLOGY

A PARTICIPATORY AND EXPERIENTIAL LEARNING APPROACH

Did you know that we learn best when we experiment or "learn by doing", reflecting and internalizing lessons from our own experience? This is why this learning guide is based on a participatory and experiential learning approach. 

What does experiential learning mean?

Experiential learning is based on the idea that learning takes place through the transformation of experience. It capitalizes on the knowledge, experiences, and abilities of participants, so that they are prepared to apply their learning in real life. 

According to David Kolb experiential learning is usually based on four main elements, which build the learning experience:

  • Concrete experience (participants encounter an experience related to their real world)
  • Reflective observation (participants reflect on the experience and analyze it)
  • Abstract conceptualization (participants consider new ideas or a modification of an existing behavior)
  • Active experimentation (learning from the previous steps, participants are able to adopt new options and create a plan that can be implemented in the future).

What is a participatory learning approach?

A participatory learning approach engages trainees as active participants. Through different kinds of participatory activities and group work sessions, participants are actively involved in the learning process and engage with critical thinking, problem solving and decision makingin contexts that are relevant to them.

This approach enables and empowers participants to share, discuss, and analyze, so that they can enhance their capacity to reflect, evaluate, and plan - in coordination with other stakeholders. Moreover, it ensures their buy-in and commitment to take action following the training.

This is why there is very little conventional "teaching" in this learning guide, and the role you will play is the one of a "training facilitator" more than of a traditional "trainer".
