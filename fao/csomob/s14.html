Session 14

HOW CAN CSO PLAY A ROLE IN THE VGGT IMPLEMENTATION PROCESS? (THE ROLE-PLAY)

Goals of this session

  • To reflect on the potential application of the VGGT to actual tenure governance challenges
  • To allow participants to explore their role as CSO representatives in addressing a tenure governance challenge
  • To identify potential roles that participants and their organizations can play to apply the VGGT at country level.

Timing

2 hours.

Clearly establish the learning objective(s) that you wish to achieve through the role-play exercise. For example: a. Analyse actors and responsibilities (including of CSOs) in improving the governance oftenure. b. Analyse processes and possible entry points for improving the governance of tenure with linksto the Voluntary Guidelines

Identify 3 to 4 scenarios relating to tenure challenges to serve as the basis for the role-plays. You canfind examples in the People’s Manual (Chapter 2). These may be adapted. Alternatively, identify a realcase from the country or make up a new scenario. (See section “What you need”)

Prepare individual briefs for each actor with an introduction to the case and an indication of howhe/she might react or behave. Ideally select the players the day before, and give them the briefs so thatthey can prepare their part in advance.

Present a few prepared presentation slides to explain the role-play session and to describe the case andthe different roles.

Carry out the role-plays.

Conduct a reflection on the role-plays using three or four prepared facilitation questions. See sample questions in the section “What you need”. The reflection could include: a. feedback to the other groups. b. individual sharing of experiences playing a specific role. c. new elements that arose during the role-playthat are normally forgotten in actual situations.

What you need

Resources

  • Guiding note: Additional background on facilitating a successful role-play
  • Role-play scenarios and participants’ briefs for the role-play (to be prepared by the facilitators)
  • Sample questions (you can find them in the guiding note)
  • People’s Manual, Chapter 2

Equipment

  • Large cards and markers to write the names of the various actors

Tips and comments

  • To make the session more interesting, prepare briefs for three or four different role-play situations. Otherwise, if time is limited, conduct the role-play just once, and ask the other participants to observeand evaluate the role-play by sharing their comments
  • Make sure the role-play scenario(s) contains the essential details, including the starting point, thecontext, and the parties involved. If the intention is to limit the range of options that participants canselect, the scenario should be very detailed. If the intention is for participants to fully explore a range ofoptions, then the scenario can be less detailed. Do not overload the orientation with unnecessary detail
  • Observers may be asked to track one particular character in the role-play or to examine a specific issue (e.g. how conflict was addressed) or may simply be asked to report back on what they saw happening during the role-play.
