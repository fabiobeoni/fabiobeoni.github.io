Session 6

PRESENTING THE AGENDA OF THE DAY

Goals of this session

  • To assess participants’ expectations in relation to the goal, objectives and content of the training.
  • To give participants an opportunity to provide suggestions during the workshop.
  • To allow the trainer/facilitator to respond in a timely manner to any issue raised during the workshop.

Timing

20 minutes for participants’ expectations, 5 minutes for the feedback poster.   

The steps

Distribute cards to each of the participants and ask him/her to write down his/her personal expectations – what they hope to learn from the workshop - one expectation per card. The cards can be stuck onto another large piece of paper.

Regroup and sum up the expectations by theme, telling participants that you will go back to them during the workshop.

Present the “What I like/Suggestions” posters and explain that they will be placed outside the workshop room, so that participants can express anonymously their views about the workshop.

Place the posters on a wall outside the room.

What you need

Equipment

  • Cards
  • Markers
  • Flip-chart paper
  • Pre-prepared poster “What I like”. If appropriate, you could draw a happy face instead of the title
  • Pre-prepared poster “Suggestions”

Tips and comments

Tips for participants’ expectations:

At the end of the workshop, return to both the learning contract and the participants’ expectations, and together with the participants, check whether both have been fulfilled. 

Tips for the feedback poster:

  • Emphasize that criticism should be constructive, and that also knowing what is going well is a great source of motivation for everyone.
  • From time to time, remind participants about the feedback poster and encourage them to write on it. Check the feedback wall at the end of each day and, when possible, address participants’ remarks during the course of the training.
  • The feedback poster can be adapted according to the local norms. For example, an anonymous suggestion box may be used or alternatively a quick oral evaluation at the end of the day requesting feedback on the main positive and negative aspects of the day.
