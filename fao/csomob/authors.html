Authors

This online learning guide has been based on “Putting the Voluntary Guidelines on Tenure into practice – A learning guide for civil society organizations”, which has been developed in partnership between several divisions of the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) and FIAN International as facilitating organization of the of the Land and Territory Working Group of the International Planning Committee for Food Sovereignty (IPC).

The methodology draws upon the one used in the People’smanual on the guidelines on governance of land, fisheries andforests: A guide for promotion, implementation, monitoring andevaluation, developed by the International Planning Committee for Food Sovereignty (IPC) and a set of publication, among which we would like to particularly acknowledge the relevance of Enhancing stakeholder participation in national forest programmes: a training manual (FAO, 2010).

The Learning Guide was tested in 2015 in several counties in Asia, Africa and Latin America by national counterparts who had a key role in assessing the guide's relevance and adding their inputs to improve the present document. These national counterparts are: LandNet in Malawi, FIAN Nepal in Nepal, Rassemblement Démocratique des Femmes du Niger (RDFN) in Niger, Fundación Guillermo Toriello in Guatemala, MASIFUNDISE Development Trust in South Africa, Transnational Institute (TNI) in Myanmar and Conseil National de Concertation et de Coopération des Ruraux (CNCR) in Senegal.
